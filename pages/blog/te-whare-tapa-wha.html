Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

This is something I've been trying to get back into the habit of doing, but I would like to go back to a yoga practise. I loved going and feeling like being there was a real treat for me, like I was really loving myself and treating myself well. While studying and working meant that I didn't always prioritise it and it fell out of my routine when deadlines loomed, which made me sad and then I started to lose motivation to go. But I understand that while I might be able to put some other things on the back-burner yoga really shouldn't be one of them.

I would really love to commit to one session of yoga a week. It doesn't have to be a full 2 hour session like I used to engage in, and it doesn't always have to be hot power yoga, I think I should be bringing in more Yin yoga into my life

Another physical activty that really brings me peace is running. Once I get past the warm-up part of the run, I start to slip into the rhythm, the pace and the whole world tends to dissappear. I have managed to keep onto one long running session a week, so I would like to continue to keep up with this! I think it's really important.

I would love to bring in more weight training sessions into my physical wellbeing. I derive a lot of my self confidence from feeling capable, both mentally and physically, so I really get that boost after a great weights session. I have been going 1-2 times a week at my peak, so I would like to continue this practise.

Finally, I walk my dog on the beach for an hour every day. This is such a lovely part of my evening, I get to connect with my partner who I may or may not have seen much that day, and get to see my dog all happy in his favourite place. In Summer, it's particularly beautiful as I get to put my feet in the ocean and walk in the waves, and that's a particular sort of pleasure I've never had before, having never lived by the ocean before.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

This is something that is new to me, I don't think I've been very good at taking care of my mental wellbeing very well at all. I have recently started introducing more hobbies into my life to put my selfworth into something other than what dress size I am. It's taken a lot of work, but I've become really proud of the strides I've been making.

To build on this, I would like to continue making time for my hobbies as I find the repetative actions in knitting and crochet to be really meditative and the sense of pride and fulfilment I get from finishing a project and thinking "man, I made that!" is amazing!

While the hobbies are great, I would also like to start journalling. I think this would be a great way to start taking stock of the day, remembering the good parts and the bad parts and challenging myself to take something positive away from even the most negative of days. I really think this will help decompress from the day, and stop my brain from running a million miles a minute when I get my head down on the pillow each night!

I think starting small in this is the best way to go (as per the advice in Atomic Habits by James Clear - great read!) and I know I struggle to form good habits, so I think if I can commit initially to a once a week decompress journal entry as a hard rule, and then if I want to add more during the week that's okay too - this will help make it a long lasting habit that I can hopefully keep for a while!
