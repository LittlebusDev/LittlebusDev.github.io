What are three new things I have learnt about myself and my ego due to core learning?

I have really cemented a few ideas about my ego and how I believe a lot of my self worth is tied up in how capable I feel when faced with a task. So when I was faced with a couple of tasks that I thought were above my current skill level, I was really worried that I was a lot dumber than my peers or even than I believed I was. This is something I have struggled with for a while, but what I've managed to learn from foundations is that I don't have to tackle the whole task in one go. Having been given the tools to help me break down a problem, I have been able to help myself filter through my emotions in terms of "okay what do I actually think is happening here, do I actually feel like I know none of this or is my ego a little bruised because I don't know as much as I think I should know by this part of the course"
I honestly think this is a skill that I will be able to take with me into bootcamp, but also hopefully into my wider life.

During the looping and listening phases, I have been able to realise that I haven't been talking about myself in the nicest way during this process. Even when explaining how I'm feeling to others during those exercises, hearing those words repeated to me, it doesn't come off as it sounds in my head and I think this is something I could be working on too. I'm in this to learn and I shouldn't me getting mad at myself or badmouthing myself. I wouldn't talk about a friend that unkindly, so why should I talk about myself that way?

Another thing I have noticed during the core sessions is that I take a lot of pride in how I am able to help others, and during this process of learning, I'm probably not going to be able to help as much as I would like to. So this will have to take some getting used to. I love being able to provide and assist in any way I can, and I'm trying to get used to the feeling that I'm the one that's needing the help. I don't know how to get used to this and I know that this will have to be something I work on, as I want to get the most out of this course and use all the tools at my disposal, and maybe other people like to help as much as I do, so I should let them...

What are the roles of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I think all of these play a vital role in programming and learning. If you don't know your values, how will you know how you can fit into a learning environment? How will you know how to work with others? How do you know yourself if you don't know your values? Empathy especially in a learning environment is crucial! Everyone learns at their own pace, everyone has different experiences that make up who they are and how they should be interacted with. Being empathetic to people will help create a safe learning environment that will ultimately benefit everyone and enable us all to learn most effectively.
This is inherantly linked to self-awareness. Being aware of yourself and what your stressors are or what your tolerance is for things will also help to cultivate a safe learning environment for yourself. It will provide you with the space you need and the tools you require to be able to learn in the most effective way.

Wha has surprised me the most about the core learning

The core learning has really helped me to create a space for myself to have a little but more of a conversation with myself and get a deeper understanding of what I need and how I will best thrive in a learning environment, but also to help me extrapolate these thoughts into a work/life environment too. Because of the core learning and the tasks set for me, I have started journalling at night (or the next morning if I couldn't do it the night before) which I'm really enjoying. This is something I've said I would do for ages, but because it's not an "enforced" thing, I've never followed through. Not that I think it's enforced here, but being able to frame it in a way like "oh it's for school" has made me take the want a little more seriously, and I have started a cute bullet journal. And It's been really theraputic to be able to sit and take something good away from the day, especially if it's been a tough learning day.

What were the most challanging aspects of the core learning

I can get very impatient, and I've been so excited about aquiring this new skill that sometimes I wish I could skip past the core sessions and get into some more coding! But when I do sit and do sit and do them, I'm always so glad I did, because I always learn a little somethinf about myself that I may not have otherwise taken the time to get to understand.

Why do I think Dev Academy, a programming school, are spending so mucg time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course

Because they're teaching us how to learn. They're giving us all the skills and self-reflection skills to be able to look at a problem and see how it fits into OUR way of thinking. It's like teaching us a new superpower. Once you understand what your limitations are, or how you as a person process a problem, or celebrate a victory, how you cope with stress, or what makes you feel capable, you're able to start building your learning plans and education wants and goals with these in mind. It's really cool honestly.

Does the time I spent studying core learning feel like a waste of time? Should I have just used the time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Absolutely not. No way. I have got a much clearer understanding of myself and how I approach problems, as well as giving me the mechanisms I need to solve the problems. I feel like I am WAY more equipped mentally to deal with the potential stressors I will face in Bootcamp than I was before. I also think that this helps everyone headed into Bootcamp because we'll all be equipped with this knowledge, which will make it so much easier to help people. If we can all accurately describe how best we need help, we're more likely to be able to get the specific help that's going to help us get the most out of the course and our learning ability. It also means inversely, if we're stressed, or reaching our limit, we're able to articulate exactly where the limit is, which means other people can take that into consideration when working with us or around us.
