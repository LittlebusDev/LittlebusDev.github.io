What is your long term goal or career pathway?

For me, a long term career pathway is headed towards an independent contractor role. I did think with my background in graphic design I would want to head into more of the UX/UI space, but I’ve really loved coding! So thinking about it this morning, I was hoping I could gear myself to the “whole” package boutique designer route, in that I could potentially take you from no brand identity or online presence, to setting you up with a logo, branding, colours, and a fully customised website. So I’d get to do all the things I’ve been really enjoying!

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

For me I would say that a strength I have is that I love learning and researching and “knock-on” effect learning which can lead me to learn some weird and wonderful things and down some great rabbit holes!

With that, comes the weakness that I often don’t know when to stop. I have really enjoyed the timer method that has been implemented here, as I really suck at setting time limitations on the fun research stage of any project.

I also tend to be a bit of a perfectionist, and the idea that something is just “good enough” rather than “perfectly as I wanted it” is something I’m trying really hard to work on!

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Personal time management and accountability for when I run over. Like I said before with my research, even if I set myself a 1 hour target for example to do some research on a topic, I would run over and then not sit down and figure out how that’s affected my timeline for the task ahead of me. I have seen people use waterfall charts and Gant charts, and they are always super aspirational to me! I think I’m better at following a pre-existing one rather than trying to create my own. Which again, is why I think the time box and timer method here is really helping me!

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: * An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives * What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

How I will manage myself to work productively and safely with other learners/facilitators/community representatives would be the way I’ve been working and learning with other people my whole life; with compassion and understanding that everyone is fighting their own fight and learning at their own pace - including myself!

What I will do when things are building up and starting to get to me would be to take a break. It's something I've been working on a lot lately, which is to pick up on my own warning signs that I may be stuggling. Here I will try and take a break, go for a coffee, get some food and a walk and see if that helps. I have also got a whole heap of hobbies that I can indulge in that help to destress! Finally, I know that my peers and the facilitators would be a wonderful resource should I need some external help in de-stressing

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

Having peers to go to for help is a big part of why I wanted to do a course in a school like setting rather than a singular online course. That would be where I would turn to first for help immediately on the Discord chat. I love how active the facilitators are on the discord page which is really encouraging and makes me feel that if I needed help I wouldn’t be interrupting their day etc so I wouldn’t be as scared to ask! I also have my dad for help who has done this course before and is working in the technical industry.
