BOOTCAMP LEARNING PLAN

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I don't think my goal has changed too much since writing this plan initially. I'm still really keen to move towards more of the boutique, one stop graphic design and wesbite creation shop for businesses. I already have a qualifaction and career history in graphic design and I've started my web development skilltree, but I'm really hoping I'll learn enough to strike out on my own and the confidence that will come with having play around with a whole bunch of JavaScript!

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in bootcamp?

My limitation will be not deep diving into the why and hows of why something works. I have some time at home to be able to go on youtube tangents and explore the minutia of why something works or different ways of doing things, and I don't think I will have the time to do this while I'm under the pump at Bootcamp. This is where my "working is good enough" mentality will have to develop!

In terms of strengths, I'm really looking forward to being able to give this everything I've got. I've confirmed to myself during foundations that I'm really driven to make the most out of this opportunity, so I'm really excited to get stuck in and revel in the challange of having this and only this to spend my days doing.

What do you think you biggest non-technical challnge at Bootcamp will be>

I touched on this a little bit in homegroup about what I think some aspect that will be a litle challanging is that I have managed to structure my day so that I can take lots of small breaks to be able to let information "settle in" and to give my eyes some break from screens and to go and "touch grass", but I'm worried that this will not be something I can do while I'm in bootcamp, but I won't know until I'm in the flow of it.

What are your expectations fro the Bootcamp team?

To me, I'm still hoping that I will be able to ask the questions I have and not feel like I'm taking up peoples time with too many questions. I like to make sure I've understood something, or have someone clarify or correct my thought processes, but I don't want to be a bother, so having someone to be able to answer my questions (like I've been told there's a "teachers table" where facilitators go when they're available to take questions) will be so helpful to me, because the stess of wondering if I'm going to be interrupting someones day will be eliminated if they're there and ready for questions.

How I'm planning to manage workload in the programme

I have noticed that small breaks are working for me, usually with learning I will sit at the computer or stare at the book until it makes sense, which oftentimes it wouldn't. So my plan us to make sure I go outside and walk, or go do some knitting, or go make a cup of tea to get myself away from the situation so that I don't get too worked up that something isn't working.

FOUNDATIONS LEARNING PLAN

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

For me, a long term career pathway is headed towards an independent contractor role. I did think with my background in graphic design I would want to head into more of the UX/UI space, but I’ve really loved coding! So thinking about it this morning, I was hoping I could gear myself to the “whole” package boutique designer route, in that I could potentially take you from no brand identity or online presence, to setting you up with a logo, branding, colours, and a fully customised website. So I’d get to do all the things I’ve been really enjoying!

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

For me I would say that a strength I have is that I love learning and researching and “knock-on” effect learning which can lead me to learn some weird and wonderful things and down some great rabbit holes!

With that, comes the weakness that I often don’t know when to stop. I have really enjoyed the timer method that has been implemented here, as I really suck at setting time limitations on the fun research stage of any project.

I also tend to be a bit of a perfectionist, and the idea that something is just “good enough” rather than “perfectly as I wanted it” is something I’m trying really hard to work on!

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Personal time management and accountability for when I run over. Like I said before with my research, even if I set myself a 1 hour target for example to do some research on a topic, I would run over and then not sit down and figure out how that’s affected my timeline for the task ahead of me. I have seen people use waterfall charts and Gant charts, and they are always super aspirational to me! I think I’m better at following a pre-existing one rather than trying to create my own. Which again, is why I think the time box and timer method here is really helping me!

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: * An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives * What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

How I will manage myself to work productively and safely with other learners/facilitators/community representatives would be the way I’ve been working and learning with other people my whole life; with compassion and understanding that everyone is fighting their own fight and learning at their own pace - including myself!

What I will do when things are building up and starting to get to me would be to take a break. It's something I've been working on a lot lately, which is to pick up on my own warning signs that I may be stuggling. Here I will try and take a break, go for a coffee, get some food and a walk and see if that helps. I have also got a whole heap of hobbies that I can indulge in that help to destress! Finally, I know that my peers and the facilitators would be a wonderful resource should I need some external help in de-stressing

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

Having peers to go to for help is a big part of why I wanted to do a course in a school like setting rather than a singular online course. That would be where I would turn to first for help immediately on the Discord chat. I love how active the facilitators are on the discord page which is really encouraging and makes me feel that if I needed help I wouldn’t be interrupting their day etc so I wouldn’t be as scared to ask! I also have my dad for help who has done this course before and is working in the technical industry.

Description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I do tend to ask a lot of questions and will sometimes need a little reassurance that it’s okay to ask the questions and that I’m not ruining peoples day by asking for some of their time. Eventually I will get over the initial reticence and it will become second nature (as it has done in previous courses) but initially some reassurance that I’m not annoying would be amazing haha

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

Nope! I have a really great support network that all understand the time commitment this course takes, and they have been wonderfully understanding of letting my home duties be a little lighter for the next few weeks!
