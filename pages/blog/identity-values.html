When have I had to make an ethical decision?

Every time we choose to recycle - that's an ethical decision.
Growing my own food - ethical decision..
I make these choices everyday

How your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I have such a supportive network around me who are helping me to realise that what I think and feel should matter to me as much as I think other peoples thoughts and feelings should matter. They’re helping me put my foot down more and stand up for what I think matters, and not back down when I can see that what I think may not align with what other people think.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

think my ability to get along with most people will really help when working in group environments! I like to think that I can help create a supportive atmosphere and help people whenever they need it.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

My limitations will be that I will often think one thing, but not often raise it if I feel it’s not worth the upset it could cause, so I tend to let things happen that I don’t particularly want to happen, just because I don’t want to upset anyone. In the past when I’ve stood up for myself and put my foot down for what I think should happen or what I think should be the way forward, I have been called “combative” because I’m usually so placid. This feeds into my fear of standing up whenever I disagree because I don’t want people to think badly of me or not want to work with me in the future.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Previously when working on a group project in my Graphic Design degree, I was asked to work in a group with a member of the class that was fully remote. We were asked to design the branding for a niche hobby app and present why we did what we did to the class. I had some ideas about how I thought I wanted it to look and pitched them to my team member, however they said they’d already done all of the design and the presentation and all I needed to do was read the script written for why “we’d” done it that way.

I didn’t say anything andread the script as they asked. At the time it wasn’t important enough to me to disrupt the process, and just let them get on with it the way they wanted. I get a little disappointed looking back on it now, because I didn’t see any of me in the project (obviously) and I missed out on an opportunity to create something I was proud of and wanted to showcase on my portfolio.


Some strategies I would try now, would be to maybe press a little harder on why they didn’t want to do the project as a group project, was it a matter of time saving? Did they have other things they wanted to get done so they’d just pressed on and got this done to clear their evening for something? maybe try and have more of a dialogue. Maybe ask if it was something about my previous design work that put them off wanting my design input on the project? Just try to get a better understanding as to why they wanted to finish the project by themselves. However if they were still insistent on not involving me in the project, I would maybe ask the teacher if I could have a try at doing it myself, or joining another team that would be willing to have input from others.
