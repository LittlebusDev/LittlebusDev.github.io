Neuroplasticity - What is it?

The brain is not a fixed, rigid quantity with no ability for movement or growth. The Little Grey Cells (tm) are able to move and grow depending on what you’re learning and how often you’re learning. This means that we are not “stuck” with the brain capacity we’re born with, and we can learn and grow and adapt!

How can we engage principles to our benefit?

For me I can use these principles to learn new skills, be that coding or sewing or pottery which will help me create things for myself and people I love around me, including a new career path. All down to the fact that I can learn, grow my brain, and solidify these ideas via practice and patience.

What are some of thats to increase your neuroplasticity?

Practise practise practise! As well as mentally reviewing or “going through the motions” of the actions that you’re trying to commit to memory or skills you’re hoping to encourage long term.

Growth Mindset What is it and why is it relevant?

Growth mindset is the belief that you can. That your only limitation in learning and bettering yourself are self imposed. Especially in classroom settings like Dev Academy, where the subject matter can be quite taxing, having that growth mindset of “I can do this, I just need to figure out how to make it work for me”

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Not really? I have done this study before during my diploma, and I have done Psychology papers, so I knew this information and the importance of engaging in learning materials with this particular mindset.

Blend with your learning plan and strategies

I would like to keep incorporating adding journalling or at least some reflection at the end of the school day, to help cement the things that I have learned that day. I also know that teaching the information I’ve learned to someone else, really helps me keep hold of the knowledge I’ve gained that day, so I would also like to keep this as part of my learning strategy
